# Local Parks — Product Brief (v1)

**City:** Chicago  
**Mandate:** City leadership funded a digital initiative to get residents outside into local parks.

## User

Primary: Chicago adults planning a **short outdoor outing today** — a walk, time with kids, a dog, or casual sport. They live near parks but often don’t know which one fits *right now*.

## Problem

Chicago has strong park access, but discovery is hard: *What’s near me? What can I do there?* Friction leads to staying home or repeating the same few spots.

## Hypotheses (v1 foundation)

Grounded in NRPA barriers with high digital relevance:

1. **Time** — If finding a nearby matching park takes minutes (not a research project), more residents will choose an outing today. The product does not create free time; it reduces **decision time**.  
2. **Awareness** — If we surface park locations and amenities clearly, residents will discover parks/offerings they didn’t know existed.  
3. **Interest match** — If residents can filter by what they want (playground, dog, sport, etc.), they’ll pick a park that fits and follow through.

## v1 outcome

Help them find a suitable park in minutes and leave with a clear next step (map / directions).

## Success

- **North Star:** more park visits over time.  
- **v1 proxies:** discovery → detail → intent (Get directions). No live foot-traffic claim.

## Scope

**In:** browse/search; filter by amenity + location (ZIP / near me); park detail (name, address, amenities); map/directions CTA.  
**Out:** accounts, bookings/payments, live attendance, program registration.

## Data

Chicago Park District **CPD_Parks** (`ejsh-fztr`). Attribution in product.

## Non-goals

Clickable discovery prototype (~90 min build) — not a full parks platform.
